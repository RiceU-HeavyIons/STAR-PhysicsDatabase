9) dNdeta

To determine the reference dNch/dη two complementary methods are used, which give very close results

First, the dNch/dy for all particles if determined from using z(&pi) (i.e. for the assumed mass of the pion). The data are then corrected using the pion efficiency. It is assumed that the pion efficiencies alone can be used as they constitute the majority of the produced particles.

Finally, the yield is corrected for the transformation: dNch/dy->dNch/d&eta

The second method uses the individual &pi, K, and p yields (dNch/dy) from the full analysis. Each particle is corrected with its own efficiency and for the transformation: dNch/dy->dNch/d&eta for each particle mass. The yields are then summed for the total (dNch/dy). Additionally, in this method, the proton and antiproton yields are corrected for Λ feeddown.
